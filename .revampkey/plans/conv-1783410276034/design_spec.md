## Research Synthesis
### Common patterns across strong dietitian websites
- A focused niche or audience is more persuasive than a generic nutrition message.
- The best sites combine trust signals such as credentials, press features, testimonials, and measurable results.
- Strong homepages quickly answer who the service is for, what problem it solves, and what action to take next.
- Content marketing is a major growth lever: recipes, blogs, guides, podcasts, and downloadable resources are common.
- Many top sites mix clinical credibility with a warm, personal founder story.

### Recommended direction for this project
- Build around a clearly defined audience rather than "everyone who wants nutrition help."
- Use a homepage structure with: hero, credibility/trust section, services, founder story, testimonials, helpful content, and strong call-to-action.
- Blend professionalism with warmth: medically credible, but visually welcoming.
- Consider adding one lead magnet early, such as a free guide, meal tips, or consultation prompt.
- Use real proof points where possible: qualifications, outcomes, media mentions, and client benefits.
